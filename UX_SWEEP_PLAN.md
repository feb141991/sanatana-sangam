# UX Sweep Plan

## Purpose
Make the app feel calmer, clearer, and more emotionally coherent without forcing novelty onto every screen.

## Principles
- Reduce friction before adding features.
- Keep major surfaces emotionally distinct.
- Favor clear next steps over crowded dashboards.
- Preserve accessibility for elders through large tap targets, strong contrast, and restrained motion.

## Priority Order
1. Panchang as a sacred-time experience
2. Kul as a living family room
3. Mandali as warm local belonging
4. Pathshala as a study sanctuary
5. Home as a daily return ritual

## Panchang
- Use an atmospheric sky-led hero tied to time of day.
- Surface sunrise, sunset, Rahu Kaal, and Abhijit as a clear sacred-time ribbon.
- Keep trust labels visible without making the screen feel clinical.
- Add gentle mobile haptics later only for meaningful actions like switching days or returning to today.

## Kul
- Make family relationships actionable, not static.
- Ensure section pages focus on one activity at a time.
- Use family-safe profile views and lineage detail sheets as the center of the experience.

## Mandali
- Reduce generic feed feeling.
- Highlight local belonging, event follow-through, and discussion warmth.
- Replace vague posting affordances with clearer intent-based actions.

## Pathshala
- Maintain strong source trust and calm reading flow.
- Promote continue-learning, recitation, and study prompts without overwhelming the reading surface.
- Keep audio and recitation authoritative and explicit about source status.

## Home
- Show fewer competing calls to action.
- Let guidance evolve from onboarding cards into one strong next-step suggestion.

## Tirtha Map
- Avoid Hindu-default labels on Sikh, Buddhist, or Jain places.
- Make place cards tradition-aware in both wording and visit cues.
- Surface the nearest few places as calm “start here” options before the larger result list.

## AI Chat
- Frame Dharma Mitra as guided reflection and study, not an unbounded chatbot.
- Use trust cues and entry lanes so people know what kinds of questions fit well here.
- Keep source-aware guidance visible without making the surface feel clinical.

## Bhakti
- Replace placeholder energy with a devotional preview that still feels alive.
- Make future scope legible: chant, remembrance, live gatherings later.
- Keep it calm and elder-friendly, not entertainment-first.

## Tracking
- Deliver the sweep section by section.
- Batch small visual consistency fixes with each major experience pass.
