# Bal Krishna Concept Batch V2

## Purpose

This batch continues the Bal Krishna reboot using the strongest results from the first generated concept round.

The goal is not to search broadly again. The goal is to refine the winning direction into a character that feels premium, sacred, childlike, and original enough to become the real Kanu foundation.

## Anchors From V1

Use these current app assets as hard reference points:

- face anchor: `/public/kanu/bal-krishna-portrait-anchor.png`
- softness support: `/public/kanu/bal-krishna-portrait-softness.png`
- body rhythm: `/public/kanu/bal-krishna-flute-hero.png`
- standing proportion baseline: `/public/kanu/bal-krishna-standing-base.png`

## What V1 Proved

- The face direction is finally working.
- The child read is strong enough to continue.
- The emotional warmth is present.
- The best image is the portrait anchor.

## What V1 Still Got Wrong

- full-body results drift toward figurine or collectible-toy energy
- crown and jewelry are too visually loud
- some body proportions are too chibi
- some poses feel posed for display, not lived or cinematic
- the child grace needs to feel more natural and less polished-doll

## V2 Priority Corrections

1. Keep the exact face family close to the portrait anchor.
2. Reduce decorative crown energy.
3. Reduce jewelry density and visual noise.
4. Keep the body clearly childlike, but less chibi.
5. Make the stance and movement feel like a real premium animated child character.
6. Keep sacred softness without turning him into a toy idol.

## V2 Design Rule

Do not improve the character by adding more detail.

Improve the character by:

- clarifying the face
- calming the ornamentation
- improving the body rhythm
- making movement and posture more believable

## Global Prompt Guardrails

Apply these to every V2 prompt:

- original Bal Krishna child hero
- preserve the face direction from the portrait anchor image
- preserve cheek softness from the softness image
- preserve graceful body rhythm from the flute hero image
- premium animated-film quality
- sacred child, not mascot
- cinematic, not collectible
- believable child posture
- restrained crown and jewelry
- dark soft curls with integrated peacock feather
- pitambar-inspired child dhoti
- peacock blue, saffron, warm ivory, muted gold

Avoid:

- toy-like proportioning
- figurine styling
- over-polished doll face
- oversized crown
- layered heavy jewelry
- adult jawline
- plastic material finish
- generic stock Krishna look
- hyper-symmetrical mannequin expression
- fake magical overload

## V2 Batch Structure

This round should generate:

- 2 face refinements
- 3 full-body refinements
- 1 premium key art refinement
- 1 expression consistency refinement

## Approval Standard For V2

Only approve outputs that satisfy all of these:

- The face still matches the v1 winner.
- The design feels more original than v1.
- The child body is less chibi and less figurine-like.
- The costume is calmer and less noisy.
- The sacred feeling comes from presence, not decoration.
- The result feels closer to a premium animated film than a collectible render.

## Target Outcome

After V2 we should have:

- 1 locked face direction
- 1 locked full-body base
- 1 usable blessing or flute hero
- 1 cleaner expression identity sheet

That will be enough to start building a real asset pipeline around the character.
